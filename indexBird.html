Places to go Birding in the Lower Mainland

These are just a few of Brian's favorite birding locations

Maplewood Flats

Maplewood Flats Conservation Area is a well maintained and easy walk for beginners. It has small ponds of fresh water as well as views of Mudlfats making it easy to see many types of birds. You can find saltwater birds and songbirds here. There is also a small post at the beginning of the trails with a bird feeding area and a poster of recent sightings.

Reifel Bird Sanctuary

Reifel is a large Sanctuary located in Delta, a 45 minute drive from metro Vancouver. This is one of Canada's top birdwatching sites and is a must see for budding birders. Wood ducks, mallards, and hooded mergansers are a constant sight to behold, but it also has rarer seasonal birds such as cranes, swans, and Snow Geese. The paths are well-maintained and there is no elevation making it easy to traverse.

Find out more!
Deer Lake

Deer Lake's beautiful scenery changes greatly from season to season. You can view large swaths of grass in the summer fully grown well over 8 feet tall. In the winter the grass dies and lays flat giving you a view of the entire area unobstructed. Bald eagles can be seen circling above, and even owls visit the area on occasion.
